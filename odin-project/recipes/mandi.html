mandi recipe

Chicken Mandi is a traditional Yemeni dish featuring tender, spiced chicken resting on a bed of aromatic basmati rice.
It is slow-cooked to lock in moisture and finished with a hot coal smoke (Dhungar) to achieve an authentic restaurant-style smoky flavor

Mandi is not only a food !

Yes, Mandi is also a major commercial and municipal center in Himachal Pradesh, nestled along the Beas River. Known as the"Varanasi of the Hills," it is celebrated for its rich heritage, ancient stone temples like the Bhutnath Temple, and stunning mountainous scenery. Acting as a crucial gateway to the Kullu Valley, the city seamlessly blends breathtaking Himalayan topography with deep spiritual significance

Mandi (or kuzhi mandi) is a beloved Middle Eastern dish originating from Yemen, famous for its incredibly tender meat and aromatic rice. Traditionally prepared in a clay tandoor dug into the ground, the meat (chicken or lamb) is slow-cooked over hot coals, allowing the rich, seasoned drippings to flavor the basmati rice bubbling below. Brought to India by Gulf returnees, it has become wildly popular in the Malabar region of Kerala and across the subcontinent.

ingredients

For the Mandi Spice Mix (Hawaij)

  • 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 1/2 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom seeds
  • 1/4 tsp cloves
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • ½ dried black lemon (Loomi)

For the Chicken marination

  • 1 lb chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp Mandi spice mix (from above)
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder (for color, optional)

For the Mandi Rice

  • 3 cups long-grain Basmati rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 dried black lemon
  • 1 bay leaf, 1 cinnamon stick, 3-4 cloves, 3 green cardamoms
  • 4.5 cups chicken stock (or water + bouillon cubes)
  • A pinch of saffron soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk or water

For the Smoke (Dhungar) & Garnish

  • 1 small piece of charcoal
  • 1 tsp ghee or oil
  • Fried onions, chopped coriander, and mint leaves for garnish

Procedure

  1. Prepare the Spice Blend

    2. Dry roast the whole spices (coriander, cumin, fennel, peppercorns, cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon) in a pan on medium-low heat for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
    Add the dried lemon and let it cool. Grind into a fine powder and mix in the turmeric.

  2. Marinate the Chicken

    3. In a bowl, combine the ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, 2 tablespoons of the prepared Mandi spice mix, oil, and chili powder. Rub this paste generously all over the chicken,
    making sure to get it under the skin. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour, or in the refrigerator overnight.

  3. Cook the Rice

    4. Heat oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot. Add the whole aromatics (bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom) and sauté for 30 seconds. Add the chopped onions and slit green chilies,
    and sauté until the onions turn golden brown. Add the soaked and drained rice, stirring gently for 1 minute to coat it in the spiced oil. Pour in the chicken stock and salt.
    Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low

  4. Steam the Chicken with the Rice

    5. Place a wire steaming rack directly over the simmering, half-cooked rice. Lay the marinated chicken pieces on the rack. Cover the pot tightly with butter paper/parchment
    and a lid (or tightly seal with aluminum foil) to trap the steam. Cook on low heat for 25–30 minutes, or until the chicken is tender and fully cooked.(Alternatively,
    you can pan-fry or bake the chicken separately and steam the rice in the oven at 180°C)

  5. Bake the Chicken (Optional step for crispy skin)

    6. Remove the chicken from the pot and brush with a little oil. Broil or bake in an oven at 200°C (or pan-fry on high) for 5–10 minutes until the skin becomes crispy and golden brown

  6. Smoke and Garnish

    7. Drizzle the saffron milk and a dash of lemon juice into the rice, then fluff it with a fork. Place the cooked chicken back on top of the rice. Heat a piece of charcoal directly on the stove until red hot.
    Place the hot coal in a small metal bowl or piece of foil in the center of the pot. Drop a teaspoon of ghee onto the coal to create smoke,
    and quickly cover the pot tightly. Let it smoke for 5 minutes to infuse the Mandi aroma.

  7. Serve

    8. Transfer the fluffy rice to a large serving platter, arrange the chicken on top, and garnish with fried onions, cashews, and raisins. Serve hot with a side of Arabic tomato salsa (Salatah Harrah) and yogurt.
