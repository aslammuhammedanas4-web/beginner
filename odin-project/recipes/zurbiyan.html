Zurbiyan Recipe

Zurbian is a fragrant, slow-cooked Yemeni rice and meat dish similar to biryani. It combines tender, spiced meat (lamb or chicken) and potatoes with fluffy basmati rice,
beautifully infused with saffron

ingredients

For the Meat Marinade & Cooking:

  • Meat: 1 kg mutton or chicken, cut into pieces
  • Potatoes: 2 - 3 large potatoes, peeled and halved
  • Yogurt: 1 cup plain yogurt (curd)
  • Onions: 4 large onions, sliced
  • Ginger-Garlic: 2 tbsp minced ginger and 2 tbsp minced garlic
  • Whole Spices: 2 bay leaves, 1-inch cinnamon stick, 4 green cardamoms, 5 cloves, and 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • Ground Spices: 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tbsp coriander powder, 1 tsp cumin powder, and 1 tsp red chili powder
  • Cooking Fat: 3 - 4 tbsp vegetable oil or ghee
  • Salt: To taste

For the Rice:

  • Basmati Rice: 1 kg basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • Saffron: A pinch of saffron soaked in 3 - 4 tbsp warm water
  • Water: For boiling the rice with a handful of whole spices (cinnamon, cloves, cardamom) and sal

procedure

  1. Fry the Onions

    2. Heat oil in a large pot. Add sliced onions and fry until they are golden brown. Remove and set aside half of the fried onions for garnish.

  2. Make the Marinade

    3. In a food processor, blend the remaining fried onions with the yogurt, ginger, garlic, and the ground spices (turmeric, coriander, cumin, and chili powder)
    until it forms a smooth, thick paste.

  3. Marinate and Sear the Meat

    4. Marinate the meat with the yogurt-onion paste and salt for at least 2 hours. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat ghee, add the whole spices,
    and sear the marinated meat on medium heat for 5 - 10 minutes

  4. Slow Cook Meat & Potatoes

    5. Add a splash of water, cover the pot tightly, and let the meat slow-cook on low heat for about 30 - 45 minutes.
    Place the potato halves into the pot and cook for an additional 20 - 25 minutes until the meat and potatoes are tender

  5. Boil the Rice

    6. In a separate pot, bring the saffron-infused water to a boil. Add the soaked basmati rice and cook until tender,
    stirring occasionally.

  6. Layer and Dum (Steam)

    7. Layer the partially cooked rice evenly over the cooked meat and potatoes. Drizzle the saffron water and melted butter
    (or ghee) over the rice. Top with the reserved crispy fried onions.

  7. Final Steam

    8. Cover the pot tightly with aluminum foil and a heavy lid. Place on the lowest flame (or a tawa/griddle) and
    let it steam/dum for 20 - 25 minutes.

  8. Serve

    9. Gently fluff the Zurbiyan with a fork, ensuring the meat and potatoes remain intact. Serve hot with raita or salad.
